Beni Suef Branch · 2022-2023 · 7 Phases · 17 Volunteers

Selecting deserving families was straightforward. The real complexity was matching each family with a viable micro-project suited to their environment, skills, and capacity to generate sustainable income.

My Role - 7 Phases

  • Project-family matching based on local market conditions
  • Pre-delivery training and rehabilitation for beneficiaries
  • Competitive procurement of equipment
  • Delivery, documentation and financial reconciliation
  • 3-month post-delivery follow-up
  • Project closure

Renewable Energy · EPC · Community Infrastructure

500 kWp Solar PV Program Across 32 Community Sites

18 Schools & 14 Mosques - New Valley Governorate · EGY Power Solar Company · Arab Renewable Energy Co. (AOI)

The Challenge

Delivering 500 kWp across 32 scattered community sites, each with its own stakeholders and schedules. Schools blocked access during student hours, materials faced supplier delays, and a back injury forced remote management for the final 20 days.

My Role

  • Site surveys across all 32 locations with full obstacle assessment
  • Daily coordination with Housing Directorate, Awqaf and Education Authority
  • Individual scheduling with each school principal and mosque imam
  • Sourced local labor and materials to cut costs
  • Secured a local warehouse for all equipment and materials
  • Coordinated supplier deliveries against contract schedule

The Critical Decisions

Front-loaded rooftop stockpiling to buy time while schools were inaccessible. Shifted to mosques and weekend windows during exam periods. With 12 days left and 9 schools remaining, built a new action plan and increased workforce to recover schedule.

Results

  • Delivered 2 days before deadline despite injury and delays
  • Punch-list cleared next day with full handover complete
  • Full financial reconciliation and progress billing completed

Impact

500 kWp of clean energy delivered to 32 community sites across New Valley Governorate, serving students and worshippers daily.

Agritech · Smart Irrigation · EU-Funded

Drip Irrigation Program - 38 Feddans, Beni Suef

Arab Office for Youth & Environment · Coptic Evangelical Organization · EU-Funded · Al-Fashn, Beni Suef

The Challenge

Working on active farmland with nervous landowners who feared any damage to existing crops and trees. Managing their anxieties was as critical as the technical execution. One day before handover, traditional irrigation flooded the site and made final inspection nearly impossible.

My Role

  • Site handover and full risk assessment across 38 feddans
  • Coordination with partner associations, EU donor, and landowners
  • Sourced local labor from the village to reduce cost and time
  • Secured warehouse for equipment and materials storage
  • Supervised land preparation, trenching and pre-installation works
  • Maintained constant field presence to manage farmer concerns
  • Coordinated supplier deliveries per agreed schedule

Team

  • 2 engineers · 5 technicians
  • 15 local workers · 2 drivers

The Critical Moment

Day before handover, farmers flooded the site with traditional irrigation, making the final inspection nearly impossible. The team worked through it and delivered on time.

Results

  • Project delivered on schedule to the supervising consultant
  • Financial claims, progress billing and budget reconciliation completed

Impact

38 feddans of agricultural land now equipped with modern drip irrigation, reducing water consumption and improving yields for farmers in Al-Fashn, Beni Suef. EU-funded.
